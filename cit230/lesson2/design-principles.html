Design Principles

PARC: Alignment

Amazon

Go to Amazon

Alignment is very prominent on Amazon's website. Looking at their home page, you can see that everything lines up perfectly with each other. For example, the yellow "Sign in" button is perfectly in align with the "Create an account" button directly underneath it. Alignment helps with the consistency of a page, and makes it more aesthetically pleasing.

PARC: Repetition

Pinterest

Go to Pinterest

Repetition helps bring balance to any website. Looking at Pinterest's website, repetition is very obvious. The only content on this website is a bunch of pictures, sometimes linked to other websites. You can see the repetition on the size of each photo; I've noticed that there are consistently two sizes of photos used on Pinterest. This helps the website not look so scattered or messy.

PARC: Contrast

National Geographic

Go to National Geographic

Contrast is, and should be, used throughout any website. This is what helps viewers read text and notice important details. on National Geographic's website, you can see that the black text on white backgrounds, or vice versa, make the words stand out more. There are also other details, such as the yellow button at the top on the black background.
